FAQ
Questions answered.
How is the money actually invested?
Contributions are invested in professionally managed ETF portfolios through our regulated investment partner. Parents choose from Conservative, Balanced, or Growth options. KISuper operates under an Australian Financial Services Licence (AFSL).
What happens if I want to close the account?
The account is designed to be held until the child turns 18, but life happens. Accounts can be closed with appropriate notice. Any outstanding investments will be liquidated and returned. Full terms are in the T&Cs.
Does the gifter need a KISuper account?
No. Anyone can gift via the shareable link — they just need a credit or debit card. No sign-up, no app, no account. That's the whole point. Making it frictionless for Grandma is the feature.
How old does my child need to be?
KISuper accounts can be opened from birth (age 0) and are designed to grow through to age 18. The earlier you start, the more time the investments have to compound.
Is there a minimum contribution?
No minimum for gift links — someone can send $5 or $5,000. For parent recurring contributions, we're targeting a $10/month minimum. Full pricing will be published before launch.
What are the fees?
We'll publish our full fee structure before launch. We're targeting a simple annual platform fee (% of balance) with no contribution fees. No hidden charges. No toy aisle price-gouging either.
